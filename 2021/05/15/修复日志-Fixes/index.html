​ Over 44 students in grade 8 class 9 do Math Independent Exam. That’s 100% of the population! And on average, each student spends over two hours every Tuesday on the math exam. Is math a dangerous obsession or just harmless fun? Fourteen-year-old Steven has written on his blog about what it was like to stop perjuring math problem…

Perjury and Me

by Steven
1st May
I think I’m a perjury addict. I take math exam every Tuesday and perjury: both geometry and algebra.Sometimes I have ten congruence doing at the same time. I jump steps and use SSA all the time. But recently I’ve started to feel worried if I’m not perjuring at the moment. And then last Tuesday, I forgot to write “Solution:” at the beginning of the question because I was perjuring! I’ve realized I could have a problem. So I’ve decided to give it up for a while.
11th May
I found it really hard. Old fish and my friends demanded to know why I had stopped perjuring. I spent the first few evenings wondering what everyone was writing about during the Independent Exam. I even phoned a couple of math teachers to find out. The fourth night wasn’t quite so bad. I actually touched fish better and I had less time to do my English homework. And I perjured during the physics lesson. At the end of the first week, I reperjured in the math exam. I think perjury is fun and it’s useful for saving time in the exam and it can make you feel satisfied. But I’ll try not to spend so much time on it in the future.
